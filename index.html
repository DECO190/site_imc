BMI

(Body mass index)

The body mass index (BMI) is an international measure used to calculate whether a person is at the ideal weight. Developed by the polymath Lambert Qu√©telet at the end of the 19th century, it is an easy and quick method for assessing the level of fat in each person, and is therefore an international predictor of obesity adopted by the World Health Organization (WHO).

What to do to improve BMI? To improve BMI it is important to check whether it is above or below what is considered normal. When the BMI is in the thin range, it is important to consult the nutritionist so that, in addition to making a complete assessment, an eating plan that focuses on weight gain in a healthy way is indicated.

On the other hand, when the BMI is in the range of overweight or obesity, it can be indicated by the nutritionist to perform a diet with more caloric restriction, in addition to the practice of regular physical activity, as it is possible to speed up the metabolism and favor weight loss, which directly influences BMI.
